When a potential client searches for "solicitor near me" on their phone at 9pm, they're probably stressed. Maybe they've just had an accident, received legal papers, or need urgent advice. The last thing they should encounter is a website that takes 11 seconds to load, displays a security warning, or can't be navigated with a screen reader.

Yet that's exactly what's happening across North Yorkshire.

A recent independent audit of 136 solicitor firm websites in the region found that **96% fail basic accessibility checks**. Not "could be better" — fail. As in, potentially in breach of the Equality Act 2010 and definitely losing clients every single day.

---

## What the Audit Found

The audit tested five critical metrics across all 136 sites. Here's what it revealed:

### Accessibility: 96% fail

Only 6 firms passed a standard automated accessibility test. The rest had issues like missing alt text, poor colour contrast, unlabelled form fields, and broken heading structures — all of which make websites unusable for people with disabilities.

Under the Equality Act 2010, service providers (including solicitors) have a duty to make reasonable adjustments for disabled people. An inaccessible website is a barrier to access. With 1 in 5 UK adults living with a disability, you're not just risking legal exposure — you're excluding 20% of your potential client base.

### Page Speed: Average mobile load time of 10.9 seconds

The average solicitor website in North Yorkshire takes nearly 11 seconds to load its main content on mobile. Google considers anything over 4 seconds "poor." Research shows users abandon pages that take longer than 3 seconds to load.

If your competitor's site loads in 2 seconds and yours takes 11, the client never sees your credentials. They've already moved on.

### SSL Certificates: 43% invalid or missing

59 of 136 sites either had expired SSL certificates, no certificate at all, or HTTPS incorrectly configured. When someone visits these sites, their browser shows a warning: "Your connection is not private."

For a solicitor handling sensitive personal and financial information, this isn't just a technical problem — it's a trust destroyer.

### Local SEO: 95% fail

Only 7 sites had proper local SEO signals in place. Most lacked LocalBusiness schema markup, didn't link to their Google Business Profile, and buried (or omitted entirely) their address and phone number.

When someone searches "conveyancing solicitor Harrogate," Google uses these signals to decide which firms to show. Without them, you're invisible.

### Mobile-Friendliness: 89% pass

This was the one bright spot. Most sites at least render correctly on mobile devices. But rendering correctly and being usable are two different things — especially when the page takes 11 seconds to load.

## Why This Matters for Your Practice

### 1. Legal Risk

The Equality Act 2010 requires service providers to make reasonable adjustments for disabled people. Website accessibility is increasingly considered a reasonable adjustment. An inaccessible website exposes your firm to potential legal action.

### 2. Lost Revenue

The "Purple Pound" — the collective spending power of disabled people and their households in the UK — is estimated at £274 billion per year. 73% of disabled online shoppers report barriers on more than a quarter of websites they visit, and many choose not to return to businesses with poor accessibility.

### 3. Client Trust

An SSL warning or a slow-loading page sends a message: "We don't take our digital presence seriously." For solicitors, trust is everything. Your website is often the first impression a potential client has of your firm.

### 4. Competitive Disadvantage

If 96% of firms in the region are failing, fixing these issues gives you an immediate competitive advantage. When your site loads in 2 seconds and your competitor's takes 11, you win that client.

## What You Can Do Right Now

Here's a quick self-audit you can run in 10 minutes:

**1. Check your SSL**  
Visit your website in Chrome. Is there a padlock icon in the address bar? If not, or if you see a warning, your certificate needs immediate attention.

**2. Test your speed**  
Go to [Google PageSpeed Insights](https://pagespeed.web.dev/) and enter your URL. Look at the mobile score and Largest Contentful Paint (LCP). If your LCP is over 4 seconds, you're losing clients.

**3. Test on your phone**  
Open your site on your mobile. Can you read the text without zooming? Can you tap buttons easily? Does it load quickly? If not, you have work to do.

**4. Check accessibility**  
Run your homepage through [WAVE](https://wave.webaim.org/). Fix any errors flagged in red — these are critical issues that affect real users.

**5. Check your local SEO**  
Use [Google's Rich Results Test](https://search.google.com/test/rich-results) on your homepage. Do you see LocalBusiness or LegalService schema? If not, you're missing a major opportunity.
