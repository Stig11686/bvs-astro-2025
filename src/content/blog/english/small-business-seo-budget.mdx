## The Truth About SEO and Small Business

SEO is one of those things that can feel like a minefield. There's so much to learn, so many tools to choose from, and so many people telling you what to do. But let's be real. You don't need to be an expert. You don't need to pay thousands of pounds for a consultant. You just need to know the basics and do them well.

SEO is about being found by the right people. It's about making sure your website is easy to find, easy to navigate, and easy to understand. And it's about making sure your content is relevant to what people are searching for. That's it. That's the core of SEO. And it's something you can do on a budget.

## What You Can Do Without Spending a Lot

Let's talk about the real, actionable stuff. Here are a few strategies that have worked for real small businesses — including one of my own clients.

### 1. Know Your Audience

You know your customers better than anyone. But do you know what they're searching for? Take a step back and think about the keywords people might use to find your business. Are they looking for a local service? Are they looking for a product? What are they trying to find?

This is where keyword research comes in. It's not about finding the most popular keywords, it's about finding the ones that are relevant to your business and that your customers are actually using. Use free tools like Google Keyword Planner, Ubersuggest, or even Google Trends to find out what people are searching for.

### 2. Optimise Your Website

Your website is your online shop window. Make sure it's clean, easy to navigate, and fast. A fast-loading website is not only better for your customers, it's also better for your SEO. Use tools like Google PageSpeed Insights to check your site's performance and make improvements.

Also, make sure your website is mobile-friendly. More people are using their phones to search, and Google now prioritises mobile-first indexing. If your site doesn't look good on a phone, you're already at a disadvantage.

### 3. Write Quality Content

Content is king. But it doesn't have to be expensive. Write about your business, your services, and your customers. Write about the problems your customers face and how your business can help. Use your blog or your website to publish this content regularly.

Make sure your content is well-structured, uses your keywords naturally, and provides real value to your readers. People are more likely to share your content if it's helpful, and that's how you get more visibility.

### 4. Build Backlinks

Backlinks are like votes of confidence. If other websites link to your site, it tells search engines that your site is trustworthy and relevant. But how do you get them? You don't need to pay for them. You can earn them by creating great content, reaching out to local businesses, or collaborating with other websites.

For example, if you're a local florist, you could reach out to local blogs or community sites and offer to write a guest post. That way, you get a backlink and you also get your name out there.
