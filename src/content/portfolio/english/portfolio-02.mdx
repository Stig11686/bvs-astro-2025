**The challenge?** Create something that captures the magic without losing focus on what matters most: the dresses.

## What We Built

The design phase involved plenty of collaboration. Elizabeth had strong ideas about imagery, and I had to incorporate those while keeping the dresses centre stage. We went through several iterations until we found the perfect balance.

### A Menu That Makes an Impression

I opted for something you don't see on most websites: **the main imagery appears in the background of the navigation menu**. When the menu opens, a transition animation reveals the striking background image.

It creates a genuine wow factor — and sets the tone for the boutique experience.

### WooCommerce Without the Checkout

We used WooCommerce to create a full dress catalogue, but with a twist:

- **No cart or checkout** — fitting is essential, so online purchasing isn't an option
- **Fixed "Book Appointment" button** — visible on screen at all times
- **Individual dress enquiries** — brides can enquire about specific dresses through product pages
- **Beautiful galleries** — every dress showcased with the attention it deserves

The technology serves the business model, not the other way around.

### Brand-Led Design

Every element reinforces Elizabeth's unique positioning:

- Dark, atmospheric colour palette
- Mystic and gothic visual touches
- One-of-a-kind dress photography
- Copy that celebrates individuality over conformity

## Key Takeaways

If you run a bridal boutique (or any business with a strong brand identity), your website needs to:

1. **Capture your unique positioning** — don't settle for templates that could be anyone's business
2. **Drive the right action** — if you need appointments, make booking effortless
3. **Let collaboration happen** — the best results come from designer and client working together

A website can feel as special as the in-store experience. It just takes the right approach.
