**The challenge?** Win work from homeowners looking for bespoke joinery AND industry professionals vetting them for contracts.

## What We Built

The design puts their work front and centre. I kept the hero text to the side, letting the stunning imagery of their craftsmanship do the talking.

### Image-Led Design

- **Large, crisp project photography** — showcasing the quality of their work
- **Neutral colour palette** — greys and clean whites that complement their brand
- **Prominent logo** — consistent with their existing printed materials
- **Clear calls-to-action** — either explore more work or book a consultation

### Custom Project Showcase

Using WordPress and Advanced Custom Fields, I created a system they can manage themselves:

- **Add new projects easily** — upload images, add details, publish
- **Multiple gallery images per project** — show different angles and details
- **Categorised work** — kitchens, staircases, fitted furniture, all organised
- **Testimonials and case studies** — proof of quality for both audiences

### E-Commerce Ready

When they were ready to sell products online, we added WooCommerce with custom product options. The store launched in mid-2025, expanding their revenue streams without needing a site rebuild.

## Key Takeaways

If you run a trades or craft business, your website needs to:

1. **Show, don't tell** — let your work speak through quality imagery
2. **Serve multiple audiences** — homeowners and trade professionals have different needs
3. **Be manageable** — you shouldn't need a developer to add a new project
4. **Scale with you** — today's brochure site might need e-commerce tomorrow

The right website positions you for higher-value work and attracts clients who appreciate quality.
