## What We Built

The brand colours — dark greens and gold — gave us a strong foundation. Combined with bold typography and stunning photography from a local photographer, the site immediately positions The Yorkshire Unicorn as a premium destination.

### Direct Booking Focus

Every design decision pushed visitors toward booking directly:

- **Prominent call-to-action buttons** — book direct, not through third parties
- **Clear room showcases** — carousels showing each room's features
- **Pricing transparency** — no need to check Booking.com for rates
- **Dog-friendly indicators** — clear icons showing which rooms welcome pets

### Easy Content Management

Using WordPress and Advanced Custom Fields, the team can manage everything themselves:

- **Add new rooms** — as they become available
- **Update local attractions** — showcase Skipton and the Dales
- **Manage amenities** — update what's included as the offering evolves
- **No developer needed** — for day-to-day content changes

### Social Proof Integration

To address the "no reviews" problem, we integrated a TripAdvisor widget that automatically displays reviews as they come in. As the business grows, so does the social proof — right on the homepage.

## Key Takeaways

If you're launching a hospitality business, your website needs to:

1. **Drive direct bookings** — every booking through your site is more profitable than through third parties
2. **Build trust quickly** — professional design and integrated reviews overcome the "new business" problem
3. **Be flexible** — new businesses evolve, and your website should evolve with you
4. **Show, don't tell** — quality photography does more than any amount of text

The right website lets you compete with established players from day one.
