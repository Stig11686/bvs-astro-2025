## How We Fix It

### 1. We Find the Problems

First, I dig into your data and watch how real people use your site:

- **Analytics review** - Where do people drop off? What pages lose them?
- **User recordings** - Watch actual visitors navigate (or struggle with) your site
- **Heatmaps** - See where people click, scroll, and ignore
- **Form analysis** - Where do people abandon your forms?
- **Competitor comparison** - What are successful competitors doing differently?

No guessing. Just evidence.

### 2. We Prioritise What Matters

Not everything needs fixing. I'll rank opportunities by:

- **Impact** - How much difference will this make?
- **Effort** - How hard is it to implement?
- **Confidence** - How sure are we this will work?

We start with high-impact, low-effort wins. Quick results build momentum.

### 3. We Test and Improve

For significant changes, we test before committing:

- **A/B testing** - Show half your visitors version A, half version B. See which wins.
- **Measure everything** - Track exactly how changes affect conversions
- **Iterate** - Keep the winners, ditch the losers, keep improving

This isn't a one-time project. It's an ongoing process of getting better.

## Common Questions

<Accordion label="How do I know if I need this?" group="faq">

If you're getting decent traffic (say, 500+ visitors a month) but your conversion rate is below 3%, there's probably room to improve. If you're not sure what your conversion rate is, that's a sign too - we should at least set up proper tracking.

</Accordion>

<Accordion label="What kind of results can I expect?" group="faq">

It depends on how broken things are now. Sites with obvious problems often see 50-100% improvement in conversion rates. Well-optimised sites might see 10-20% gains. I'll give you realistic expectations based on your specific situation.

</Accordion>

<Accordion label="How long until I see results?" group="faq">

Quick wins can show results within weeks. Bigger changes need more traffic to prove they're working - usually 4-8 weeks minimum. Ongoing optimisation compounds over time; the best results come from sustained effort.

</Accordion>

<Accordion label="Do you guarantee results?" group="faq">

I can't guarantee specific numbers because too many factors are outside my control (your traffic quality, your offer, your market). But I only recommend changes I believe will improve things, and I track everything so we know what's working.

</Accordion>

<Accordion label="What if my website needs rebuilding?" group="faq">

Then we should probably do that first. I'll tell you honestly if optimising your current site makes sense or if you'd get better ROI from starting fresh. Sometimes it's better to rebuild with conversion in mind from the start.

</Accordion>

<Accordion label="Can you help with ads and traffic too?" group="faq">

My focus is making your website convert better. I can recommend partners for paid ads, SEO, or other traffic generation. Often it makes sense to fix conversion first - there's no point paying for more traffic to a site that doesn't convert.

</Accordion>

## The Maths That Matters

Let's say you're a service business with these numbers:

- **1,000 visitors/month**
- **2% conversion rate** (20 enquiries)
- **25% close rate** (5 new customers)
- **£2,000 average job value**
- **Monthly revenue from website: £10,000**

Now let's improve conversion rate to 4%:

- **1,000 visitors/month** (same traffic)
- **4% conversion rate** (40 enquiries)
- **25% close rate** (10 new customers)
- **£2,000 average job value**
- **Monthly revenue from website: £20,000**

**That's an extra £10,000/month from the same traffic.**

Even a modest improvement pays for itself many times over.
