## The 7 Most Common Reasons Your Website Isn't Generating Leads

Here's what I find when I audit a website that gets traffic but not enquiries:

### 1. Your phone number and contact details are hard to find

This sounds obvious, but it's the most common issue I see. Visitors shouldn't have to hunt for a way to contact you. Your phone number should be visible at the top of every page, without scrolling. If it's only in the footer or on the contact page, you're losing people.

### 2. There's no clear next step

What do you want a visitor to do? Call you? Fill in a form? Book an appointment? If that action isn't prominent and obvious, most people won't take it. Every page of your website needs a clear, visible call to action.

### 3. Your contact form is broken (and you don't know it)

This happens more often than you'd think. A plugin update, a server change, an email going to spam — and suddenly enquiries are disappearing into the void. One of the first things I check in any audit is whether the contact form is actually delivering messages. For Ilkley Dental Care, fixing this alone was transformative.

### 4. Visitors don't trust you yet

A visitor who doesn't know you needs to see evidence that you're legitimate and that others have had a good experience. That means reviews, testimonials, case studies, and professional presentation. If those signals are missing or buried, people leave before they get in touch.

### 5. Your website talks about you, not your customer

Most business websites lead with 'what we do' and 'who we are'. But visitors arrive with a problem they need solved. If your homepage doesn't quickly show them that you understand their situation and can help, they'll move on. The most effective websites lead with the customer's problem, not the business's CV.

### 6. It's slow or broken on mobile

More than half of your visitors are probably on a phone. If your site loads slowly, looks wrong, or is difficult to use on mobile, a significant chunk of your potential leads are bouncing before they see anything. This is a technical issue, but it has a direct commercial impact.

### 7. You're not showing up in search at all

Sometimes the problem isn't conversion — it's visibility. If potential customers are searching for what you offer in Yorkshire but your site doesn't appear, no amount of conversion optimisation will help. A basic local SEO foundation makes a significant difference for most service businesses.
