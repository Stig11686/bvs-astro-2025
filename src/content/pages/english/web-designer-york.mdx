## Common Questions from York Business Owners

<Accordion label="How much does a website cost in York?" group="faq">

A brochure site typically starts from around £1,500. E-commerce or more complex builds (booking systems, catalogues, membership areas) will be more. I'll give you a clear, itemised quote after our first conversation — no vague estimates.

</Accordion>

<Accordion label="Do I need a local web designer, or does it matter where you're based?" group="faq">

For most projects, location matters less than it used to — I work with clients across Yorkshire and beyond via video call. That said, I'm 25 miles from York and happy to meet in person if you'd prefer. Many clients find it reassuring, especially at the start of a project.

</Accordion>

<Accordion label="How long does a website take to build?" group="faq">

A typical brochure site takes 4–6 weeks from sign-off to launch. More complex builds may take longer. I'll give you a realistic timeline upfront — and stick to it.

</Accordion>

<Accordion label="Will my website rank on Google?" group="faq">

I build every site with solid on-page SEO foundations — proper structure, page speed, meta data, schema markup. Getting to page one takes ongoing effort and depends on your market, but you won't be starting from scratch.

</Accordion>

<Accordion label="Can you help if I already have a website that's not performing?" group="faq">

Yes — this is one of the most common things I'm asked. My Website Audit service gives you a clear, prioritised list of what's holding your site back. Some clients fix the issues themselves; others have me do it. Either way, you'll know exactly what the problems are.

</Accordion>

<Accordion label="What about ongoing costs?" group="faq">

WordPress itself is free. You'll need hosting (I offer managed hosting from £35/month as part of a care plan, which also covers security, backups and updates). Domain names are typically £10–15/year.

</Accordion>

<Accordion label="Do you work with businesses outside York?" group="faq">

Yes. I work with businesses across North Yorkshire and beyond. York is a key area for me, but I have clients in Skipton, Ilkley, Harrogate, Leeds and further afield.

</Accordion>

<Accordion label="How is working with a freelancer different from working with a York agency?" group="faq">

Agencies have overheads — offices, account managers, multiple layers of people. That's reflected in their pricing. With me, you get the same quality of thinking and execution without the markup, and you deal with one person who genuinely cares about your results.

</Accordion>
